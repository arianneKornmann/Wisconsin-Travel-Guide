Entertainment
The Milwaukee Art Museum

The Milwaukee art museum collects and preserves 30,000 pieces of extraordinary artwork. The museum gets over 400,000 visitors each year. They have been collecting art for 125 years. The Milwaukee Art museum’s main mission is education. They offer involving classes, tours, and a full calendar of events for all ages.

Olbrich Botanical Gardens

Olbrich Botanical Gardens was created by a Madison attorney, Michael B. Olbrich. He purchased land to develop the park and flower garden in 1921. Their mission is to “Enrich lives & inspire people to live in harmony with the natural world”. The gardens include 16 acres of outdoor gardens and a big conservatory to explore.

Henry Vilas Zoo

Henry Vilas Zoo opened to the public in 1911 and has continued to add exhibits and attractions. They are proud to be one of the only a few admission-free, community supported zoos across the country! It is one of the ten remaining free zoos in North America. Their mission is “to conserve and protect the wonders of the natural living world”.

House on the Rock

The House on the Rock was opened to the public in 1960 by Alex Jordan who built his dream house on top of a chimney rock. Over the years he added many collections, displays, galleries and exhibits. If you plan to go here, reserve a few hours of your day due to the amount of fascinating stuff to see. Some of the main attractions at the House on the Rock include the world’s largest carousel, a 200-foot sea creature and automated music machines.

Lambeau Field

Have a fan of football in the family? Lambeau field is the home field of the Green Bay Packers! The Packers have won 13 league championships, 9 pre-super bowl NFL titles and 4 super bowl victories. Stadium tours are available. You can also take a visit to the Packers Hall of Fame, Packers Pro Shop, Johnsonville Tailgate Village, and the 1919 Kitchen & Tap.
